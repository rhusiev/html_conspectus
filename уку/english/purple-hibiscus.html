Purple Hibiscus

Purple Hibiscus

Jul 20, 20267 min read

by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

Preview-read thoughts

Mama tries to calm everybody though. She feels bad too, but still has strength to help others too

Papa is a businessman, they have a chambermaid => pretty rich

Aunty Ifeoma is the opposite to Papa

A child? Did it die? We don’t hear about it in previous chapter - it did die in an accident

Is it ok in that culture to change partners?
Again so much Papa praising(for not betraying his wife this time), as if he was god-like

Seems that Jaja and the heroine(Kambili) are really friends: want to have lunch together, understand each other without words

A line of Nigeria as a country is also being told: lots of coups by military, bribes, ministers stealing the money… and at the same time people longing for freedom
There isn’t enough money for teachers but they still work(of course not everyone)

Standard Criticising the government => the editor has been arrested

The editor is the one that I associate with(or at least want to be in this story): he defies the not legitimate authority, is tortured, but still insists on the right.
At the same time he is smiley and friendly, cares for his children

About Papa’s desire of perfection:
God is both love and law, if you remove anything He isn’t God any more

Such respect for grandparents:
“because every man who was old enough to be called grandfather deserved to be greeted by his grandchildren”
Probably because life isn’t long in Africa

It was sinful for Mama to bend before Igwe
At the same time it was sinful for Kambili not to kneel and kiss bishop’s ring, he is “a man of God”
Both were Christians, but bishop had a church rank
Double standards

In the end nobody(even Mama) truly follows the beliefs of Eugene, they just do it without his consent:
Lying to Grandpa, going to the festival, eating before Mass
Kevin also bribes a policeman when Papa isn’t in the car

Aunty lives in a poorer place, where everything is different than in theirs
At the same time many differences aren’t caused by richness but by a difference in mentality: nothing is allowed, everything is restricted by Papa(even though money can allow everything) and everything is free with Aunty, but restricted by money

While being at aunt Jaja becomes a lot more free, Kambili only a bit
They feel they aren’t like everyone, start thinking and talking

How can Papa call himself a christian, while doing so much harm to his children?
Why do everyone in church tolerate?

How did everything become the same again, after that accident? Why did Kambili wonder about Jaja protesting his father?

“It was what Aunty Ifeoma did to my cousins, I realized then, setting higher and higher jumps for them in the way she talked to them, in what she expected of them. She did it all the time believing they would scale the rod. And they did. It was different for Jaja and me. We did not scale the rod because we believed we could, we scaled it because we were terrified that we couldn’t.” - p158

It seems that Kambili and Jaja became somewhat less close than before, they look at each other less and don’t speak a lot

Knows Bible well:
“I remembered my own confirmation, how Papa had said I was a bride, Christ’s bride, and I had been surprised because I thought the Church was Christ’s bride.”
So Papa taught his children, not only demanded

Jaja seems to disbelieve everything, only because of a bad example set by his father. He didn’t go to communion and Aokpe

The story somehow transforms from the story of a bad father to a story about broken heart

After Papa’s death it may become easier, but at the same time harder: Jaja in the jail, broken mentally etc

Jaja again doesn’t see any point in his life, except for defending others

Characters

Papa

Eugene
Always sat in the front pew, monitoring the others
⇓
Thinks that laws, security etc are more important than freedom, humaneness
⇓
Obliges to use Latin
Tries himself to be perfect and expects everyone to be
Especially his kids, even if they are small, it is not good for their mental health
“Because God has given you much, he expects much from you. He expects perfection.”
It doesn’t work this way!

He doesn’t seem to really love God and people but rather obeys laws and traditions(still believes in correctness of everything though, like pharisee)
He often gives money to the poor, but no emotions are shown
Later: it seems that he loves his family(crying when punishing) and other people(regretting about story, because of which Ade was killed)

Therefore he is furious when HIS son doesn’t obey
He probably has always demanded a lot from his children, so now Jaja wanted to rebel

“but not too loudly so they would not sound like the mushroom Pentecostal churches” - again, like pharisees

He also speaks English in public, thinks that Nigerian culture is worse than foreign, also again to look good in others’ eyes

He loves his kids though(comes to Kambili, when she is sick; hugs her tight, when Mama is a hospital)

He also risks by criticising the new government after a coup, so he truly believes what he says

Several times uses violence to his family(Jaja’s finger, an accident when Kambili had a period, pouring a tub with boiled water, kicking because of the painting) and then cries
He can’t understand how to combine love with justice

Experiences a lot of stress due to the death of the editor, children being not obedient, fearing about the future of the country and his family etc. In the end he’s no longer that strong man

Kambili

“intelligent beyond her years, quiet and responsible” - as her form mistress said

Is still a child that isn’t prepared for difficulties, doesn’t know what to do when mother feels bad or when Jaja doesn’t obey

Wants love from father, but can’t meet his high expectations
Doesn’t believe in herself, can’t take the initiative: neither in group activities(is frightened when asked to sing) nor in conversations(doesn’t talk to anyone)
“I was awkward and tongue-tied”

Imagines everything in details(her mom with blood, underground office etc)

Feels everything deeply: can’t focus on written words, because always sees Mama’s blood and dead brother’s soul

Does everything to please her father, not because it’s right
Constant “I wish I said that first”

Imagines God as white British man
Again, the mentality of colonized

Becomes less self-conscious after a visit to Aunty, joins a volleyball team

After the trauma made by Papa, becomes even more confident in herself, ask questions
“I knew I would not have asked before. I would have wondered about it, but I would not have asked.”

Aunty Ifeoma

Has political views similar to Eugene’s, but isn’t so “religious”. Doesn’t create arbitrary rules for herself and others, wants freedom

Amaka

At first thinks that Kambili and Jaja are typical kids of rich parents, that know nothing about the world but put themselves above others
But after some period of time she understands that they suffer from domestic violence and aren’t happy
So she decides do help Kambili become less shy and locked, understanding her mom’s intentions

She is a real patriot, doesn’t want Nigeria to remain a colony - doesn’t want to be baptised with English name. She made up with beautiful Igbo names that glorify God(so it’s not against Him), but doesn’t want foreign one(because it means that Nigeria is inferior)
