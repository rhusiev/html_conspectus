Appendix: converting LoKr into sequence of linear layers

By the definition of Kronicker product for C∈Rup​×uq​ and D∈Rvp​×vq​:

\begin{flalign} C \otimes D &= \begin{pmatrix} c_{11} & c_{12} & \dots & c_{1u_q} \\ c_{21} & c_{22} & \dots & c_{2u_q} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ c_{u_p1} & c_{u_p2} & \dots & c_{u_pu_q} \end{pmatrix} \otimes \begin{pmatrix} d_{11} & d_{12} & \dots & d_{1v_q} \\ d_{21} & d_{22} & \dots & d_{2v_q} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ d_{v_p1} & d_{v_p2} & \dots & d_{v_pv_q} \end{pmatrix} = &\\ &= \begin{pmatrix} c_{11}D & c_{12}D & \dots & c_{1u_q}D \\ c_{21}D & c_{22}D & \dots & c_{2u_q}D \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ c_{u_p1}D & c_{u_p2}D & \dots & c_{u_pu_q}D \end{pmatrix} = &\\ &= \begin{pmatrix} c_{11}d_{11} & c_{11}d_{12} & \dots & c_{11}d_{1v_q} & c_{12}d_{11} & \dots & c_{12}d_{1v_q} & c_{12}d_{11} & \dots & c_{1u_q}d_{11} & \dots & c_{1u_q}d_{1v_q} \\ c_{11}d_{21} & c_{11}d_{22} & \dots & c_{11}d_{2v_q} & c_{12}d_{21} & \dots & c_{12}d_{2v_q} & c_{12}d_{21} & \dots & c_{1u_q}d_{21} & \dots & c_{1u_q}d_{2v_q} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ c_{11}d_{v_p1} & c_{11}d_{v_p2} & \dots & c_{11}d_{v_pv_q} & c_{12}d_{v_p1} & \dots & c_{12}d_{v_pv_q} & c_{12}d_{v_p1} & \dots & c_{1u_q}d_{v_p1} & \dots & c_{1u_q}d_{v_pv_q} \\ c_{21}d_{11} & c_{21}d_{12} & \dots & c_{21}d_{1v_q} & c_{22}d_{11} & \dots & c_{22}d_{1v_q} & c_{22}d_{11} & \dots & c_{2u_q}d_{11} & \dots & c_{2u_q}d_{1v_q} \\ c_{21}d_{21} & c_{21}d_{22} & \dots & c_{21}d_{2v_q} & c_{22}d_{21} & \dots & c_{22}d_{2v_q} & c_{22}d_{21} & \dots & c_{2u_q}d_{21} & \dots & c_{2u_q}d_{2v_q} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ c_{21}d_{v_p1} & c_{21}d_{v_p2} & \dots & c_{21}d_{v_pv_q} & c_{22}d_{v_p1} & \dots & c_{22}d_{v_pv_q} & c_{22}d_{v_p1} & \dots & c_{2u_q}d_{v_p1} & \dots & c_{2u_q}d_{v_pv_q} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ c_{u_p1}d_{v_p1} & c_{u_p1}d_{v_p2} & \dots & c_{u_p1}d_{v_pv_q} & c_{u_p2}d_{v_p1} & \dots & c_{u_p2}d_{v_pv_q} & c_{u_p2}d_{v_p1} & \dots & c_{u_pu_q}d_{v_p1} & \dots & c_{u_pu_q}d_{v_pv_q} \end{pmatrix} \end{flalign}

We will just consider the case of C⊗D, and the case where D is further split into BA naturally follows from this.

As an input we get b×t×q, where b - batch size, t - number of tokens and q - embedding size

To simplify things, let us just consider a single token of a single batch:

\begin{flalign} & \mathbf{x} = \begin{pmatrix} x_{1} \\ x_{2} \\ \vdots \\ x_{q} \end{pmatrix} \end{flalign}

Let us see what is the result of just multiplying x and C⊗D:

\begin{flalign} & (C \otimes D) \mathbf{x} = \begin{pmatrix} c_{11}d_{11}x_{1} + c_{11}d_{12}x_{2} + \dots + c_{11}d_{1v_q}x_{v_q} + c_{12}d_{11}x_{v_q+1} + \dots + c_{1u_q}d_{1v_q}x_{q} \\ c_{11}d_{21}x_{1} + c_{11}d_{22}x_{2} + \dots + c_{11}d_{2v_q}x_{v_q} + c_{12}d_{21}x_{v_q+1} + \dots + c_{1u_q}d_{2v_q}x_{q} \\ \vdots \\ c_{11}d_{v_p1}x_{1} + c_{11}d_{v_p2}x_{2} + \dots + c_{11}d_{v_pv_q}x_{v_q} + c_{12}d_{v_p1}x_{v_q+1} + \dots + c_{1u_q}d_{v_pv_q}x_{q} \\ c_{21}d_{11}x_{1} + c_{21}d_{12}x_{2} + \dots + c_{21}d_{1v_q}x_{v_q} + c_{22}d_{11}x_{v_q+1} + \dots + c_{2u_q}d_{1v_q}x_{q} \\ \vdots \\ \vdots \\ c_{u_p1}d_{v_p1}x_{1} + c_{u_p1}d_{v_p2}x_{2} + \dots + c_{u_p1}d_{v_pv_q}x_{v_q} + c_{u_p2}d_{v_p1}x_{v_q+1} + \dots + c_{u_pu_q}d_{v_pv_q}x_{q} \end{pmatrix} \end{flalign}

We can see that there is a pattern: in each line d index increments by one every time, and every vq​ times the d index is reset. Therefore, the first vq​ elements of x are dotted with the whole row of D and multiplied by the first element of C, the next vq​ elements of x are dotted with the whole row of D and multiplied by an element from C. For example, the first vq​ elements of the first row’s sum can be rewritten: c11​⋅D[1][:] x[1..qv​], the next vq​ elements as c12​⋅D[1][:] x[vq+1​..2vq​]; the first vq​ elements of the second row as c11​D[2][:] x[1..vq​], the next vq​ elements as c12​D[2][:] x[vq+1​..2vq​], etc.

This means that we can rewrite the above expression as:

\begin{flalign} & \begin{pmatrix} c_{11}D[1][:]\ \mathbf{x}[1..v_q] + c_{12}D[1][:]\ \mathbf{x}[v_{q+1}..2v_q] + \dots + c_{1u_q}D[1][:]\ \mathbf{x}[((u_q-1)v_q+1)..u_qv_q] \\ c_{11}D[2][:]\ \mathbf{x}[1..v_q] + c_{12}D[2][:]\ \mathbf{x}[v_{q+1}..2v_q] + \dots + c_{1u_q}D[2][:]\ \mathbf{x}[((u_q-1)v_q+1)..u_qv_q] \\ \vdots \\ \vdots \\ c_{u_p1}D[v_p][:]\ \mathbf{x}[1..v_q] + c_{u_p2}D[v_p][:]\ \mathbf{x}[v_{q+1}..2v_q] + \dots + c_{u_pu_q}D[v_p][:]\ \mathbf{x}[((u_q-1)v_q+1)..u_qv_q] \end{pmatrix} \end{flalign}

We tend to take some parts of x, to be specific every vq​ element at once. We will rewrite x as a vq​×uq​ matrix:

\begin{flalign} & \mathbf{x'} = \begin{pmatrix} x_1 & x_{v_q+1} & \dots & x_{(u_q-1)v_q+1} \\ x_2 & x_{v_q+2} & \dots & x_{(u_q-1)v_q+2} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{v_q} & x_{2v_q} & \dots & x_{u_qv_q} \end{pmatrix} \end{flalign}

Now we can rewrite that matrix as:

\begin{flalign} & \begin{pmatrix} c_{11}D[1][:]\ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{12}D[1][:]\ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{1u_q}D[1][:]\ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \\ c_{11}D[2][:]\ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{12}D[2][:]\ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{1u_q}D[2][:]\ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \\ \vdots \\ \vdots \\ c_{u_p1}D[v_p][:]\ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{u_p2}D[v_p][:]\ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{u_pu_q}D[v_p][:]\ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \end{pmatrix} = \heartsuit \end{flalign}

Now let us go from the other end and see, what Dx′ is:

\begin{flalign} & D \mathbf{x'} = \begin{pmatrix} d_{11} & \dots & d_{1v_q} \\ d_{21} & \dots & d_{2v_q} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ d_{v_p1} & \dots & d_{v_pv_q} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x_1 & x_{v_q+1} & \dots & x_{(u_q-1)v_q+1} \\ x_2 & x_{v_q+2} & \dots & x_{(u_q-1)v_q+2} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{v_q} & x_{2v_q} & \dots & x_{u_qv_q} \end{pmatrix} = &\\ & = \begin{pmatrix} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] & D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] & \dots & D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \\ D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] & D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] & \dots & D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ D[v_p][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] & D[v_p][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] & \dots & D[v_p][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \end{pmatrix} \end{flalign} \begin{flalign} & (D \mathbf{x'})^T = \begin{pmatrix} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] & D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] & \dots & D[v_p][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] \\ D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] & D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] & \dots & D[v_p][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] & D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] & \dots & D[v_p][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \end{pmatrix} \end{flalign} \begin{flalign} & C (D \mathbf{x'})^T = \begin{pmatrix} c_{11} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{12} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{1u_q} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] & c_{11} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{12} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{1u_q} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] & \dots\\ c_{21} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{22} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{2u_q} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] & c_{21} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{22} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{2u_q} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] & \dots\\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots\\ c_{u_p1} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{u_p2} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{u_pu_q} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] & c_{u_p1} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{u_p2} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{u_pu_q} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] & \dots \end{pmatrix} \end{flalign}

Now, if we concatinate the second row to the first, the third to them, etc… (flatten the matrix), we will get:

\begin{flalign} & \begin{pmatrix} c_{11} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{12} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{1u_q} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \\ c_{11} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{12} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{1u_q} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \\ \vdots \\ c_{21} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{22} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{2u_q} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \\ \vdots \\ \vdots \\ c_{u_p1} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{u_p2} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{u_pu_q} D[1][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \\ \vdots \\ c_{u_p1} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][1] + c_{u_p2} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][2] + \dots + c_{u_pu_q} D[2][:] \ \mathbf{x'}[:][u_q] \\ \end{pmatrix} \end{flalign}

We can see that this is the same as ♡.

If we denote turning x into x′ as reshape(x), and flattening of the matrix as flatten(M), we can write the above as:

\begin{flalign} & \heartsuit = flatten(C \cdot (D \cdot reshape(\mathbf{x}))^T) \end{flalign}
