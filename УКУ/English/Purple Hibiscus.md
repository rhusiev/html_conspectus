## Characters
### Papa
**Eugene**
Always sat in the front pew, monitoring the others
$\Downarrow$
Thinks that laws, security etc are more important than freedom, humaneness
$\Downarrow$
Obliges to use [[Латинська мова|Latin]]
Tries himself to be perfect and expects everyone to be
Especially his kids, even if they are small, it is not good for their mental health
"Because God has given you much, he expects much from you. He expects perfection."
It doesn't work this way!

He doesn't seem to really love [[Бог|God]] and people but rather obeys laws and traditions(still believes in correctness of everything though, like pharisee)
He often gives money to the poor, but no emotions are shown
Later: it seems that he loves his family(crying when punishing) and other people(regretting about story, because of which Ade was killed)

Therefore he is furious when HIS son doesn't obey
He probably has always demanded a lot from his children, so now *Jaja* wanted to rebel

"but not too loudly so they would not sound like the mushroom Pentecostal churches" - again, like pharisees

He also speaks English in public, thinks that Nigerian culture is worse than foreign, also again to look good in others' eyes

He loves his kids though(comes to [[Purple Hibiscus#Kambili|Kambili]], when she is sick; hugs her tight, when Mama is a hospital)

He also risks by criticising the new government after a coup, so he truly believes what he says

Several times uses violence to his family(Jaja's finger, an accident when Kambili had a period, pouring a tub with boiled water, kicking because of the painting) and then cries
He can't understand how to combine love with justice

Experiences a lot of stress due to the death of the editor, children being not obedient, fearing about the future of the country and his family etc. In the end he's no longer that strong man

### Kambili
"**intelligent** beyond her years, **quiet and responsible**" - as her form mistress said

Is still a child that **isn't prepared for difficulties**, doesn't know what to do when mother feels bad or when *Jaja* doesn't obey

Wants love from father, but can't meet his high expectations
**Doesn't believe in herself**, can't take the initiative: neither in group activities(is frightened when asked to sing) nor in conversations(doesn't talk to anyone)
"I was awkward and tongue-tied"

Imagines everything in details(her mom with blood, underground office etc)

**Feels** everything deeply: can't focus on written words, because always sees Mama's blood and dead brother's soul

Does everything **to please her father, not because it's right**
Constant "I wish I said that first"

Imagines God as white British man
Again, the mentality of colonized

Becomes less self-conscious after a visit to Aunty, joins a volleyball team

After the trauma made by [[Purple Hibiscus#Papa|Papa]], becomes even more confident in herself, ask questions
"I knew I would not have asked before. I would have wondered about it, but I would not have asked."

### Aunty Ifeoma
Has political views similar to [[Purple Hibiscus#Papa|Eugene's]], but isn't so "religious". Doesn't create arbitrary rules for herself and others, wants freedom

### Amaka
At first thinks that Kambili and Jaja are typical kids of rich parents, that know nothing about the world but put themselves above others
But after some period of time she understands that they suffer from domestic violence and aren't happy
So she decides do help Kambili become less shy and locked, understanding her mom's intentions

She is a real patriot, doesn't want [[Нігерія|Nigeria]] to remain a colony - doesn't want to be baptised with English name. She made up with beautiful Igbo names that glorify God(so it's not against Him), but doesn't want foreign one(because it means that [[Нігерія|Nigeria]] is inferior)
