Ralph and Piggy act pretty rational at the beginning, trying to organise everything, especially find a way into civilisation
They are only 12 though. They act pretty mature for their age, being able to survive for a few weeks

Ralph and Piggy are civilisation's representatives on the uninhabited island, while Jack shows everything beast-like in human nature 

It seems that Jack is the biggest problem on the island, he wants fun, doesn't think about future and seeks power
Later Ralph and Piggy, though being the opposite to him, basically do the same
They seem more reasonable, but are still able to provoke conflicts, Ralph wants to hold the power, competing with Jack even in reckless and useless actions. They even join the feast and are engaged in killing Simon
The most reasonable person seems to be Simon, not being part of either group and understanding that beast is in boys' heads

There are no actions committed by small kids, they are still not able to make their own decisions

Ralph - civilisation
Piggy - science
Jack - instincts
Simon - wisdom, the only reasonable person there

At first during reading I don't see childish behaviour often, as even adults could have behaved the same(maybe not as savage, but still)

It seems like a childish behaviour, when the boys start believing in the beast and Jack makes a new group, disconnecting from the main one, to kill it. Later they raid Ralph's group and even imprison and kill some kids, destroying the civilisation's principles and becoming more savage
It is terrifying, because I can imagine adults doing the same

When the officer appears, the civilisation takes power again, suppressing the savage. However, it is still somewhere there and in every human
