# In English

Finally, Summer has arrived! Two exams are behind me, and only one remains. It's the "Programming Basics" exam, and I doubt I'll be able to prepare for it anyway. Sooo... That means I can finally go back to some of my projects.

I look over at Bogdan. As always, he's passionately typing another masterpiece on his little laptop.

Hmmm, what should I do? I haven't had so much freedom for a long time. Ah, yes, the site! A few weeks ago, I thought I had done my part of that project. However, when Bogdan tried to use it, there were many more bugs to fix. As I don't have any chores, I have to finish what I promised.

We started working on it over a year ago, and the code looks terrible now. As a result, my desire to look into the problem dropped instantly.

Ding. I looked at the phone. Oh, my saviour! Liza needs some help!

While waiting for her to come, I wrote a few lines of code here and there. Fortunately, she arrived quickly, and we could change focus to something else. Unfortunately, her code didn't look any better than mine, and we got stuck for the next hour trying to understand the incomprehensible.

"Hey, you forgot the brackets there!" Bogdan shouted.

Oh, come on! At least it solved the problem.

Now that it's gone, it's time to go home. That promised project can wait for another day.

As always, Bogdan and I walk together. He doesn't live far from me, so we often go home, discussing various topics. The conversation flows from space and oceans to university and personal projects, going through unbelievable AIs and our uncertain future. It ends with us standing at a crossroads for fifteen minutes or so, not wanting to end it. But there's no rush today, and I can let my imagination wander where it wants.

"Wow, a train!" These are no ordinary trains -- kids drive them. I haven't seen them in a long time and thought they were abandoned.

"Yeah, it's summer, so they are more active now."

Right, summer. It's still hard to believe that the semester is over.

As we continue our stroll, the freshness of the air fills our souls, and the brightness of the blue-blue sky reaches the ends of the expanse. No cloud dares to cover the sun. As I inhale, my body cools, feeling relaxed and ready for new challenges.

This time, the conversation starts with that project I successfully avoided today, but, as always, AI takes over. Another brilliant idea comes to Bogdan's mind.

"Do you remember what I want my laptop to do when I open the lid?"

"Umm..."

"I mean... I want it to be secure but at the same time easy..."

"Oh, right! You wanted to use the camera to recognise your face and unlock the computer! And if during five seconds after the lid is opened, if it does not see your face, it locks itself."

"Exactly!"

"By the way, why don't you just implement fingerprint detection? It is much simpler and more reliable."

"Well, yes, but I don't have a fingerprint scanner..."

"You can buy a Yubikey. Not only can you unlock your device with its fingerprint detector, but also use it as 2FA."

"Huh, maybe. By the way, my dad has one."

"Really? I wouldn't expect it, especially in Ukraine."

"Moreover, his company has fingerprint detectors to enter and exit. Once, he took me and my brother to the office, but then the signalisation went off for some reason, resulting in him not being able to enter the building afterwards. There is a secret door, though, and he used it instead."

"OK, I'll remember that, thank you."

As I was saying it, a railway intersected the path. After we cross it, we'll pass a small unpleasant loud part, but the reward will be a nice park.

"Hey," said Bogdan, "let's go that way today!" He pointed to the right, where a small pathway ran.

"Sure, no homework -- no rush. Except for English."

The path is narrow, but the trees on both sides create a pleasant atmosphere. Charming sounds of birds come from everywhere as we continue the stroll, surrounded by trees, flowers, and nature.

"Can't believe we are in the city," said Bogdan.

"Yeah, it's so quiet here."

And indeed, the trees are so dense that the city is not visible. Only the tips of the tallest buildings can be seen from afar. In addition to buzzing and singing around, it creates a feeling of a village: somewhere near the city but separated from it.

As we move forward, an open lawn with a crossroad appears. And the moment we step out of the shade, it immediately becomes clear that we're still in Lviv. Accepting Bogdan's offer, we choose the right path. It should lead to Horikhovyi Hai, which is already familiar to us.

We enter the shadows again, and the noise of the city disappears. The only thing that can be heard is our steps and birds singing. The trees are so dense that the sky is not visible.

After following the path for a while, we reach a road which seems familiar to me. I look around, and, indeed, we used this path once. I recall that time. Uhh, that was unpleasant. It was supposed to be a new way that did not need the crossing of the railway. However, it turned out not to have paved roads. Instead, there were a lot of humps. Thankfully, the horrible part is on that side of the railroad, so there is nothing to worry about now.

Only now do I realise that this place is not that calm. A railway is still on the left, so peace won't last long.

"I have friends that live right next to the railway," I say, "and there is peace there, too, until a train appears. They can't do anything then, as the ground trembles."

"Oh, right. We have been going through this quiet path for a while now, but if there was a train, there would be no peace here."

I remember that Bogdan lives not too far away from a railway too.

"By the way, do you hear trains at home?"

"Sometimes, but not too often. However, when the war just started, people were not allowed to go outside after 12. On the other hand, trains started going even more often, so a careful listener could hear their screeching from afar."

"Wow..."

"Oh, look! A stump!"

"What?.. Huh?.."

"Once, I did a photogrammetry of this stump! Do you know what it is?"

"Umm... Probably... Something related to 3D?"

"Yes! It is a technique that allows you to create a 3D model from a set of photos. I took a bunch of photos of this stump and then used a program to create a 3D model of it," said Bogdan, as always being Bogdan.

"Seriously? Doing it with a stump is just... genius."

"It was so realistic that it just didn't fit the simulated environment. Well, that's kinda the point. Wait, where are you going? Do you want to already go up? However, it is probably faster for you to use that stroll."

"I don't know, you lead me. We can explore today as much as we want, as we don't have any homework left! Except for English... I even don't know where to start."

"Yeah... Oh!"

"What?"

"Let's write about today's stroll!"

"Aha, that's a good idea! We'll be writing about us walking until the moment we decide to write about us walking!"

"Exactly! Our writings will go side by side, finishing in metawriting about the writing itself. I think Matthew will like it!"

"Yeah. I'll even write how I struggle during the programming exam, looking blankly at the screen and recalling the stroll."

And there was I, sitting in front of the computer, seeing meaningless letters running on the monitor. They wanted something; they needed attention. Another task, another error... I knew I had to change something, but the number of tries had already increased so much that I did not care. Once excited about everything, Bogdan does not spark joy and is a mere reflection of the past self. The memories of that walk come to mind, but I shove them away and gather all my strength to face the task. But the mission is accomplished, and the material is ready.

Now the only thing left is to sit down and start writing about a clear sunny day, when the sky was blue, and the air was fresh; about how there was no homework left, except English, and how we went for a walk to a park...
