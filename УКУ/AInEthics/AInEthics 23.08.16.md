# Approach
Approach in EU to AI:
- Safety & Fundamental rights
- Research & Industrial capacity

Also high emphasis on small and medium enterprises(SMEs) - don't want monopolies

Risk-based: the higher risk for smth - the higher regulation
But for lower risks - lower regulations
In order for small and medium enterprises to have some chances

Better do it safely and preserve rights than have a rapid development

Have already given >200b eur to digitalise the society in order for them to understand what is going on with technologies

Also supporting innovations: doing hubs

# Proposal
Prohibited(not for personal use or research):
- Circumvent person's consciousness or other vulnerabilities using AI to do harm(Physical or Psychological)
- Mass surveillance
- "Real-time" remote biometric identification in public space for law enforcement, except for very special cases approved in advance or extreme urgency. It also means that if it is done after everything, not real-time - allowed

## Risk levels
Unacceptable risk(prohibited):
- Cognitive behavioural manipulation
- Social score
- Real-time and remote biometric identification

High risk(conformity assessment. It should be checked by the authority before being deployed):
- access to employment, education and public services
- safety components of vehicles
- law enforcement
- critical infrastructure

Limited risk(be transparent that you do this):
- biometric categorization
- Chatbots
- Impersonation
- deepfake

Minimal risk(no obligation) - the rest
